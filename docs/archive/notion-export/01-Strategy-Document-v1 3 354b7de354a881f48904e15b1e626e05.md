Arcwright is building the infrastructure for adaptive experiences: an API for stories, games, and simulations that respond to the people inside them, and the games that prove what becomes possible. Arcwright Studios is the consumer-facing publishing sub-brand for games inside the broader Arcwright identity.

## 2. Strategic Pillars

The six commitments that shape how decisions get made when the situation is ambiguous.

**1. Earned-evolution.** The company moves through three phases (creative studio, then studio plus platform, then platform-first), and each phase justifies the next. Phases are gated by proof signals, not calendar.

**2. Founder-controlled and profit-first.** 51%+ founder voting control indefinitely. 51%+ economic ownership through capital-efficient growth as long as feasible. Investors and board members provide leverage, expertise, accountability, and network value, but they do not have unilateral power to redirect the mission, force an exit, remove the founder, or override core strategy without founder consent. Board seats are earned by meaningful contribution, not granted as permanent authority.

**3. Platform and games both get protected time.** If one is starving the other, course-correct in monthly review. Neither is sacrificed to the other by default. The attention-allocation risk is real and named.

**4. Human-led, AI-augmented.** Humans are the authors and players are co-authors; AI is the runtime that serves them. This is a structural commitment, not a current-state belief. The platform empowers storytellers; it does not replace them.

**5. Quality and ambition over scale and speed.** Don't ship sanded-down work. Don't get rigid on one version of the idea. Exhaust effort on innovation, creativity, and ambition. The honor code: a commercial failure with ambitious work is preferable to a commercial success with compromised work.

**6. Build for unrepeatability.** The platform's reason to exist is to give interactive media a property tabletop has always had: the property of "you had to be there." Every play session genuinely unrepeatable.

## 3. Three-Horizon Roadmap

Phases are gated by proof signals. Year ranges are illustrative for planning, not commitments.

### Horizon 1 (target: 6-12 months, gated by proof signals)

**Identity:** Creative studio with internal infrastructure. Pixar-shape.

Each horizon represents a distinct business with a different buyer, user, and GTM motion. These are sequential, not parallel: Horizon 1 builds one consumer game and one internal engine. It does not build a developer platform or an enterprise product. Those belong to a different company phase that the current company is trying to earn the right to enter. The company's attention and build capacity belong entirely to H1 until proof signals authorize the next phase.

**Primary work:**

- Build the platform MVP (API-first, optional dashboard, code-first SDK, hosted API as core).
- Ship Nightcap (working title, pending trademark clearance), the AI-powered murder mystery party game, as the wedge product.
- Build while employed full-time.
- Bootstrap. No outside capital.

**Phase exit criteria:** Going full-time, see Section 7. Horizon 1 to Horizon 2 transition uses the same base criteria with additional Horizon 2 signals layered on (TBD).

### Horizon 2 (target: 12-24 months, gated by proof signals)

**Identity:** Studio plus platform. Valve-shape.

**Primary work:**

- Refine and expand platform based on internal use and early external interest.
- Open platform to a small set of external developers (closed beta, then early access).
- Develop the monster RPG with role-shifting career paths (TBD likely Horizon 2).
- Begin enterprise wedge: team building and corporate events (combined) as the first commercial vertical for B2B platform sales.
- Likely first co-founder or senior hire (role TBD; deferred decision based on which gap is most painful when proof signals hit).
- Capital: optionally a strategic angel round of $100K-$500K from operator angels for 5-10% total, only if it accelerates timeline and stays founder-controlled.

**Phase exit criteria:** Combination of (a) external developer adoption, (b) recurring revenue, and (c) enterprise contract value. Specific thresholds TBD.

### Horizon 3+ (target: 24+ months, no upper bound, gated by proof signals)

**Identity:** Platform-first with continued creative output. Stripe-shape, with creative studio still active.

**Primary work:**

- Scale platform adoption. Many third-party developers shipping on the infrastructure.
- Expand verticals (see Section 4 for pipeline list).
- Launch mission-aligned pricing tier within the for-profit company for under-resourced institutions, education, cultural. Exact structure and qualification criteria TBD.
- Capital: optionally a small seed round of $1M-$3M for 15-20% if accelerating enterprise pull is strategic. Series A only if and when scaling on founder's terms with real revenue and proof. Default assumption: no Series A unless something specific changes the calculus.
- Continue shipping creative work. The studio function never dies even as the platform becomes the larger business.

## 4. Vertical Strategy

**Horizon 1 wedge:** Adult social party games. Nightcap is the wedge product. Single vertical focus through Horizon 1 to maintain build velocity and clarity.

First buyer persona: hosts of adult game nights and dinner parties; groups of 4 to 8 adults comfortable using phones at the table, willing to pay for novelty and replayability, motivated to replay because their social circle changes and the experience produces different outcomes each time. This is the first customer. "Adult social party games" is the market category; the host is the customer.

**Horizon 2 enterprise exploration:** Enterprise is a portfolio of arc templates running on the same Arcwright platform, not a separate platform. The current enterprise wedge hypothesis is intentionally draft: white-label-first through events vendors, direct-secondary to HR or People Ops. ICP specificity is required before serious enterprise buildout: buyer persona, decision-making unit, and buying motion must be validated rather than assumed. Enterprise buyer and vendor interviews using consumer demo footage should begin in H1 as soon as a working prototype exists, but engineering commitment to white-label features waits for signal.

**Horizon 2-3 watchlist (not in active planning until Horizon 3+, and only after the H2 enterprise wedge is validated):** Interactive fiction, kids' entertainment, museums and cultural institutions, consumer education and language learning, medical training simulations, tabletop RPG tools. These are on the watchlist because the platform's architecture could support them; they are not in the roadmap and receive no active planning or build attention until H3+.

**Watchlist (mention but don't actively build for):** Enterprise training (corporate, legal, compliance), patient education, marketing experiences, tourism.

**Declined for now:** Clinical healthcare, K-12 institutional sales, therapy as clinical product. Revisit only with a strategic partner who handles regulation.

## 5. Capital Strategy

**Founder control definition.** Founder-controlled means preserving 51%+ founder voting control indefinitely, while aiming to maintain 51%+ economic ownership through capital-efficient growth as long as feasible. Investors and board members should provide real leverage, expertise, accountability, and network value, but they should not have unilateral power to redirect the mission, force an exit, remove the founder, or override core strategy without founder consent. Board seats should be earned by meaningful contribution, not granted as permanent authority to low-value, hard-to-remove stakeholders.

### Capital staging

**Stage 0: Bootstrap.** Through Horizon 1. No outside capital. Build while employed. Fund development from personal income. Go full-time only when proof signals justify.

**Stage 1 (optional): Strategic angel round or revenue-based financing.** $100K-$500K from operator angels for 5-10% total, OR equivalent revenue-based financing if terms preserve control. Triggered only if (a) proof signals are met and (b) a specific opportunity exists that capital meaningfully accelerates. Operator angels means people who've built relevant companies and bring meaningful network and expertise. Founder-friendly terms only: no preferred shares with control rights, no board seats with veto power, no participation rights that compound.

**Stage 2 (optional): Small seed.** $1M-$3M for 15-20%. Triggered only if accelerating enterprise pull is strategic and the unit economics on enterprise are clear. Must preserve founder voting control. Likely Horizon 2 to early Horizon 3.

**Stage 3+: Series A and beyond.** Only if and when scaling on founder's terms with real revenue and real proof. Default assumption: no Series A unless something specific changes the calculus. The company is built to be profitable and sustainable without it.

**Mission-aligned pricing tier.** Subsidized pricing tier within the for-profit company for mission-aligned customers (under-resourced institutions, education, cultural). Deferred to Horizon 3+. Exact structure and qualification criteria TBD.

## 6. Team and Hiring Philosophy

**Solo through MVP.** Founder builds alone through Horizon 1. No co-founder pre-MVP. Co-founders before MVP add coordination overhead without proportionate value, and the wrong co-founder is harder to remove than they are to add.

**Skills founder covers:** Engineering, data and ML, product strategy, enterprise sales motion (sales engineering, not pure sales), and story direction.

**Identified gaps:**

- World-class visual design and animation.
- Professional narrative craft at scale.
- Marketing and community, especially developer marketing.

### Sequencing

**Horizon 1.** Default to contractors for visual design, narrative writing, marketing, and specialty technical skills. Equity-bearing collaborators are an exception, evaluated case-by-case with deliberate written justification, vesting schedule, and reverse-vesting clause.

**Horizon 2 (post-MVP, gated by proof signals).** First real co-founder or senior hire. Role to be decided based on which gap is most painful when proof signals hit (creative director, technical co-founder, or GTM co-founder are the candidate roles). Decision is made deliberately before talking to candidates, not while talking to them.

**Horizon 2-3.** Subsequent hires based on which proof signals are strongest at the time.

### Hiring principles

- Hire only when a specific gap is demonstrably blocking progress.
- Prefer contractors over employees in Horizon 1 to preserve optionality and capital efficiency.
- Hire for craft conviction, not for resume.
- All early hires should be aligned on the founder-controlled philosophy.
- Equity for early hires reflects contribution risk and timing, not standard tables.

## 7. Proof Signal Framework

The two-axis framework for go/no-go decisions on going full-time and Horizon transitions.

### Product signals (any 2 of 3)

**Build proof.** MVP is shipped, working, and stable enough for real use. Not just demoable; actually playable end-to-end with real groups.

**Use proof.** 5+ live sessions with real groups (not friends-and-family) where the session is finished and the group expresses genuine, unprompted enthusiasm. "It worked" is not enough; "they wanted more" is the bar.

**Pull proof.** Someone outside the immediate circle wants in. An unsolicited request to play, to be early-access, to partner, or to buy. Pull means demand is reaching toward the company, not the other way around. A first-channel hypothesis must be formulated and tested before any public launch: the specific channel expected to generate pull (likely social video demos of real sessions in progress, niche community embeds in game-night and mystery enthusiast communities, and founder-led direct outreach to hosts). The specific channel must be tested before public launch, not after.

### Business signals (any 2 of 3)

**Willingness-to-pay signal.** Real money paid, or clearly offered with concrete intent. Free trials, "I'd love to try it," and Kickstarter-style pre-orders without commitment do not count. Preferred H1 monetization model: host-pays-per-session or host-buys-a-session-credit-pack. Individual player subscriptions are deferred. The willingness-to-pay signal should be tested with the host-pays model at a price point that exceeds the ~$30 static murder mystery kit alternative, since that is the primary price anchor in the consumer category.

**Enterprise conversation signal.** 3+ discovery calls with prospective enterprise buyers, with at least one explicit "I would pilot this." Not "interesting concept," not "let's talk again." A specific pilot intent.

**Inbound signal.** Something organic happened that wasn't manufactured. A press mention, an unsolicited intro, a community post, a developer asking for the API. A thing that came to you, not a thing you caused.

### Decision rule

**Going full-time:** Trigger when 2 of 3 product signals AND 2 of 3 business signals are achieved. Both categories must hit; one is not enough.

**Horizon 1 to Horizon 2 transition:** Same base criteria as going full-time, plus Horizon 2-specific signals (TBD; likely includes second product in development and first paying enterprise customer). Going full-time and entering Horizon 2 are not necessarily the same event; there is a window of full-time-but-still-Horizon-1 where the founder works full-time on the existing wedge before earning the right to expand scope.

**Horizon 2 to Horizon 3+ transition:** Combination of (a) external developer adoption, (b) recurring revenue, and (c) enterprise contract value. Specific thresholds TBD.

### Implicit corollary

Partial signals after 12 months are data, not failure. The honest read is either (a) the product needs more iteration, (b) the market is different than hypothesized, or (c) the part-time pace is the bottleneck. Each has different implications. Monthly review is where this gets evaluated.
