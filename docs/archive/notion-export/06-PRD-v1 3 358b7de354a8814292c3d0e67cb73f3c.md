## 1. What We Are Building and Why

Interactive media has a ceiling that has never been broken. Stories are authored for a generic audience. Games are balanced for an average player. Every person who buys a ticket to the same movie sees the same film.

Tabletop roleplaying games cracked this problem decades ago. A skilled GM holds the arc in their head, reads every person at the table, and makes the session unrepeatable for that specific group on that specific night. The experience moves people because it was made for them, in real time, by a human who was paying attention. The problem is that this scales to almost nobody. It requires a trained facilitator, hours of preparation, and a group patient enough to learn a system before they can play.

Arcwright automates the facilitation layer without removing human authorship. The arc is still designed by a person: the plot skeleton, the characters, the world rules, the constraints on what can and cannot happen. The engine makes that arc alive for the specific group inside it, reading who is in the room and producing an experience that could not have happened with any other combination of people. Every session is unrepeatable. No GM required.

The commercial opportunity is not just party games. The same infrastructure that runs a murder mystery also runs a corporate team-building event, a museum installation, a language-learning simulation, or a monster-catching RPG with procedurally-generated worlds. This is platform territory. But platforms without proof are vaporware. The games are the proof: each one stress-tests the engine against a genuinely different design problem, and each one ships as a real product with real players.

Arcwright builds both. The platform is the primary commercial product. The games are how the platform earns the right to exist.

## 2. North Star Vision

Arcwright's maturity unfolds in three phases, each of which earns the right to the next. Phase gates are proof signals, not calendar dates.

**Horizon 1 (target: 6-12 months): Creative studio with internal infrastructure.** The company looks like Pixar: a creative studio that built its own technology because nothing off the shelf could do what it needed. Nightcap ships as a real product. The Arcwright engine powers it. The platform is not yet available to external developers; it is proven in production by the company's own work. The team is effectively one person.

**Horizon 2 (target: 12-24 months): Studio plus platform.** The company looks like early Valve: still making excellent games, but the engine is becoming its own thing. The monster RPG ships. The enterprise team-building product enters the market. A small closed beta opens to external developers who have been specifically recruited, not a public API launch. The first co-founder or senior hire joins, in whichever role is most painfully absent when the H1 proof signals hit.

**Horizon 3+ (target: 24+ months): Platform-first with active studio.** The company looks like Stripe, but the creative studio never shuts down. The platform is the primary revenue engine. External developers ship experiences on Arcwright that Arcwright did not build. The studio continues producing games that set the quality bar and push the platform's capability ceiling. The games are now also the best marketing the platform has.

At full maturity: Arcwright is the default infrastructure layer for developers building adaptive experiences, regardless of genre. The company is known among developers for the platform and among players for the games. Neither identity cannibalizes the other.

## 3. Platform Architecture Principles

These eight principles govern every technical decision. When a proposed requirement conflicts with one of these, the principle wins unless the founder explicitly overrides it with documented rationale.

**1. Surface agnosticism.** The engine has no opinion about what displays its output. It produces content events tagged with context: who the event is for, what triggered it, what the current session state is. The game layer built on top of the platform decides whether that event renders on a television, a phone, a web browser, a voice interface, or something that does not exist yet. Nightcap's TV-plus-phone setup is a Nightcap decision, not a platform decision. The platform never knows what a television is.

**2. Human arc primacy.** AI is the runtime personalization layer, not the creative author. Every experience running on Arcwright has a human-designed arc: the structural spine that defines what kind of experience this is, what the constraints are, what must happen and what can vary. The engine makes each session of that arc unrepeatable for the specific group inside it.

AI generation is called only after the arc execution layer has resolved the current session state deterministically. The LLM composes narrative from resolved, structured state data; it does not manage, infer, or update session state itself. State transitions are always deterministic. Any proposed feature that requires the AI to determine what has happened in a session, rather than express what the arc execution layer has already resolved programmatically, is a platform architecture violation and must be redesigned before implementation.

**3. Configurable composition.** Developers define each element of their arc as either authored (fixed) or generative (AI-driven at runtime). In Nightcap, the arc structure is fixed but the killer identity, character personalities, clue distribution, plot twists, and pacing are generative. In the monster RPG, the world rules are fixed but the player's story, the NPCs they encounter, and the world they explore are generative. This per-element composition dial is the core product differentiator.

**4. Unified character model.** Every character in an Arcwright session, whether controlled by a human player or driven by the AI, is the same type of object in the platform. Each character has an identity, a personality profile, goals, a knowledge state, and a relationship graph. What differs is the behavior source: AI characters have a behavior engine; human characters have an input channel.

**5. Knowledge graph as first-class infrastructure.** Every session maintains a knowledge graph: a structured record of who knows what, when they learned it, and from whom. AI character responses are automatically constrained by that character's knowledge state. This is always included in the platform, not an optional add-on. Schema complexity is what is tiered by plan.

**6. Cost-aware architecture from day one.** The platform routes AI model calls internally based on task type and quality tier. Classification and safety tasks route to small, fast, cheap models, including open source models served via managed inference providers (Groq, Fireworks, Together AI). Generation tasks route to capable models appropriate for the quality tier. Developers do not specify models; they specify task type and quality expectation. Chat 6 must calculate the per-session cost break-even between managed API pricing and open source self-hosting at projected session volumes before any self-hosting infrastructure is designed.

Two additional requirements apply from the first production session. First, routing is budget-first by default: runtime narrative generation defaults to the most cost-effective model meeting the quality tier for the task, not to the best available model. Frontier models are reserved for specific, documented, high-complexity arc junctions where the quality improvement is observable and justified. The routing default is cost-optimized-first; escalation to higher-cost models requires explicit justification in the routing configuration. Second, prompt caching is a named architectural requirement, not an assumed optimization: repeated system and context layers across a session (arc schema, character profiles, session rules, rolling session history) must be cached at every model provider that supports it. Cached input tokens cost a fraction of standard input tokens. Failing to cache repeated context in a session-based product wastes a predictable and avoidable portion of per-session AI cost from the first deployment. Chat 6 must specify which context layers are cached, at which providers, and how cache invalidation is handled when session state changes.

**7. Progressive proprietary infrastructure.** The platform launches on third-party managed AI services and replaces components with proprietary or open source self-hosted infrastructure as session volume, data quality, and economics justify each transition.

- *Tier 1, built at MVP:* Deterministic infrastructure with no AI cost: arc execution engine, session state management, knowledge graph, multi-surface event routing, developer API, usage tracking.
- *Tier 2, triggered by volume and cost pressure (Horizon 2 at earliest):* Specialized models fine-tuned on Arcwright session data. Requires thousands of structured, labeled sessions. Open source base models (Llama, Mistral, Phi) are the fine-tuning targets. Do not trigger before data and economics justify it.
- *Tier 3, never:* Foundation model development, compute infrastructure ownership.

Every completed session is a data asset. Session telemetry, instrumented from the first production session, is the mechanism that makes the Tier 2 transition possible. Competitors using the same APIs cannot replicate Arcwright-specific session data.

**8. Provider-agnostic model routing.** No platform operation has a hard dependency on any specific AI provider, model name, or model version. Every model call routes through an internal abstraction layer that maps task type and quality tier to the current best available option. This enables cost optimization through provider switching, compliance flexibility for enterprise customers, and the Tier 2 migration without changing any arc execution logic.

## 4. Nightcap: Reference Implementation v1

Nightcap is an AI-powered murder mystery party game for 4-10 players. One player among the group is secretly the killer. Every session assigns the killer randomly. The arc structure is fixed; the content is generated fresh for each group.

**Platform capabilities Nightcap stress-tests:** Multi-character ensemble management across a structured arc with a fixed endpoint; per-player knowledge state enforcement; dynamic pacing; AI-assigned role distribution with behavioral consequence; two-surface content routing via the Jackbox model.

**Arc skeleton (human-authored, fixed):** A murder has occurred before the session begins. The group is a set of characters with designed relationships and conflicting motives. The session has three structured beats: introduction and motive establishment, investigation and clue distribution, and final accusation and reveal. The killer is AI-assigned per session. The reveal is a designed story moment.

**Generative layer (AI-driven, unique per session):** Killer identity and behavioral profile; character personalities and relationship tensions calibrated to the specific group; clue content, distribution sequence, and targeting; plot twists and misdirections based on group progress; narrator dialogue and atmosphere.

**Player experience requirements:** No app install required. Onboarding under two minutes. Each player's phone functions as their private game surface. A shared display carries the narrator and group-visible story moments. The host controls session pacing.

**Session persistence:** On interruption, the platform saves character state and knowledge state. On resume, the arc restarts from the nearest designed beat. No session restarts from the beginning.

**Success criteria:**

**Qualitative gate (required to consider v1 shipped):** Five or more completed sessions with real groups outside the founder's immediate circle where players finish the full arc and express unprompted enthusiasm. "It worked" is not the bar. "They wanted to play again" is the bar.

**Personalization perception gate (required alongside qualitative gate):** In at least two of the five qualifying sessions, at least one player says or does something unprompted that specifically references a personalized detail, not a generic arc beat. A player who says "that was fun" does not satisfy this gate. A player who reacts to a specific character tension, clue detail, or plot element that was clearly calibrated to their group does satisfy it. If the qualitative bar is met but the personalization perception gate is not, the platform's personalization layer is not working at the product level regardless of technical correctness. That result requires investigation and diagnosis before scope expands.

**Player count scope for first qualifying sessions:** Initial proof sessions target 4 to 6 players, not the full 4 to 10 range. Expanding to 4 to 10 players is a post-first-proof milestone. The 4 to 6 player range reduces session coordination complexity, reduces per-session AI cost, and produces a cleaner diagnostic signal from early playtests before the system is hardened. The session design and arc structure should accommodate 4 to 10 from the beginning, but the first qualifying sessions are run with smaller groups.

**Quantitative instruments (not the gate):** Session completion rate, arc pacing variance, killer concealment rate, replay intent.

## 5. Future Reference Implementations

These are not scoped for MVP. Every significant platform architecture decision must be validated against them before it is committed. Each game is a deliberate platform stress-test, selected partly based on which capabilities it forces the platform to develop that prior games did not cover. Each game's design brief must include an explicit list of platform capabilities it is intended to validate. If a proposed game validates nothing new about the platform, it is a creative decision only and should be labeled as such.

**Monster RPG (Horizon 2, design TBD in Chat 8).** An emergent narrative experience with a designed world but no designed story. Platform capabilities this validates that Nightcap does not: world state persistence independent of any individual session; procedural generation at the world layer in addition to generative AI at the narrative layer; dynamic difficulty adjustment; player-defined motivation inference; simultaneous multi-player world state with no single designed endpoint.

**Enterprise Team-Building (Horizon 2).** A portfolio of enterprise arc templates running on the same Arcwright platform that powers Nightcap consumer. Arcs may be purpose-built for leadership development, team formation, onboarding narratives, or adapted from Nightcap structure with corporate-context content. The platform is shared; the products are distinct. Platform capabilities this validates: developer-configurable content rails strict enough for enterprise HR contexts without platform-level changes, multi-team session architecture, and debrief beat as a designed arc element type.

**Third game (selection deferred).** Should be selected deliberately against the platform capability gaps that Nightcap and the monster RPG do not cover. Candidates: asynchronous play, very short sessions under ten minutes, solo adaptive experiences, real-time competitive mechanics. Selection criterion is which gap in the platform's validated capability set is most important to close before opening to external developers.

## 6. User and Developer Experience Requirements

**Developer experience.** At H1, a developer building on Arcwright receives a REST API with the knowledge graph as the headline primitive, a TypeScript web SDK auto-generated from OpenAPI, a hosted dashboard with Visual Storyworld Phase 1 inspection surfaces, documentation leading with knowledge graph value proposition, and Nightcap as the public reference implementation. The API accepts arc definitions as structured input and returns content events during session runtime. It exposes endpoints for session management, character management, knowledge state management, and content event streaming. The dashboard serves inspection, arc monitoring, and usage management before becoming a full visual editor. At H2 prep, Unity SDK, voice/audio SDK helpers, onboarding flow, and sample projects are staged. At H2 closed beta, Unreal SDK is added if demand signal exists, with multi-tenant features. At H3 plus, full Visual Storyworld phases, marketplace if pull exists, and AI-assisted authoring are pursued.

**Player experience (via Nightcap).** No account required. No app install required; phone browser is sufficient. Session join via code or QR under 30 seconds. Private information on the player's device must never appear on the shared display by design. Character identity and personality are delivered at session start.

**Host experience (via Nightcap).** Session creation and onboarding managed through the host interface on the shared display. Host can pause, advance manually, or allow the engine to drive pacing. Host has visibility into which players have received which clues without seeing clue content belonging to other characters. Host can remove a dropped player.

## 7. What the Platform Must Do

**Session management:** Create, start, interrupt, resume, and end sessions. Emit content events tagged with target audience. Save state on interruption; restore from nearest designed beat on resume.

**Character management:** Maintain a unified character object for every participant. Generate AI character dialogue consistent with personality, goals, and knowledge state. Maintain ensemble coherence across a session arc.

**Knowledge graph:** Track per-character knowledge state throughout the session. Enforce that AI responses never reference information outside that character's knowledge state. Support configurable schema complexity.

**Arc execution:** Execute generative arc layers according to the developer-defined composition model. Adapt pacing based on session signals. Enforce authored constraints regardless of AI generation output.

**Safety:** Enforce platform-level hard stops at the model call layer, not the output filter layer. Serve developer-configurable content rails. Log safety filter activations. Make safety configuration visible in the dashboard.

**Cost and billing:** Track AI credit consumption per session, per arc, per developer account. Route model calls through the provider-agnostic abstraction layer. Enforce plan-tier limits and trigger overage or upgrade flows. Expose usage data for per-session gross margin calculation.

**Session telemetry:** Log structured telemetry for every session from the first production deployment. Minimum signals: arc beat engagement duration, pacing intervention triggers and outcomes, content event delivery confirmations, knowledge state constraint activations, session completion status, replay intent indicators.

## 8. What the Platform Must Not Do

- Must not assume a specific delivery surface at the engine layer.
- Must not treat AI as the creative author.
- Must not allow knowledge state management to be skipped or made optional.
- Must not expose model routing decisions or model names to end players.
- Must not hard-code a dependency on any specific AI provider, model, or model version.
- Must not be designed in a way that requires a rebuild to support emergent narrative arcs (monster RPG) or multi-team enterprise sessions.
- Must not launch without content safety architecture active.
- Must not assume a specific team size or build capacity in its requirements.
- Must not include authoring capabilities that only Nightcap needs and classify them as platform capabilities.
- Must not ship without session telemetry instrumented.

## 9. MVP Definition and Milestone Map

**Scope clarity:** "Platform MVP" at this stage means the internal engine that runs Nightcap, with architecture clean and documented enough that a technical co-founder joining post-MVP can understand every decision without a guided tour. It is not an externally packaged API product, not a polished developer-facing service, and not a platform open to third-party developers. The done-criterion "documented well enough for a technical co-founder" means internal architecture documentation, not a public developer experience. This distinction determines where build time goes during H1.

**MVP is done when all of the following are true:** Nightcap is playable end-to-end by a group of 4-10 real players not involved in building it. The session completes through all three beats including the reveal. Knowledge state enforcement works. Session persistence works. The provider-agnostic model routing abstraction layer is in place. Structured session telemetry is live logging minimum required signals. The platform API is documented well enough for a technical co-founder to read the architecture without explanation. AI credit tracking is live and the founder knows per-session gross margin at each player count. Content safety rails are active. At least one arc schema other than Nightcap has been designed. At least one adversarial playtest round has been completed, specifically designed to probe content safety rails, dark-content edge cases, and player attempts to break tone, embarrass other players, or inject prohibited content; results reviewed and blocking issues resolved before first real-user sessions begin. The solo-founder critical path has been honored: the developer-facing dashboard and arc authoring interface are the lowest-priority MVP components and are deferred until the consumer experience is validated; the critical path for solo delivery is arc execution engine, session management, knowledge graph, Nightcap game integration, safety rails, basic cost tracking, and structured telemetry.

**H3+ North Star:** Arcwright is platform-first. External developers ship experiences on the infrastructure. Tier 2 proprietary infrastructure is active for at least some model call categories.

**H2 gate:** External developer closed beta opens only after two gate conditions are met: at least five external developers are building non-Nightcap experiences and Nightcap has produced a profitability signal. The specific profitability threshold (revenue level, unit economics, recurring revenue, or combined signal) is deferred until Nightcap pricing is set. Monster RPG shipped or in final development. Enterprise team-building product has at least one paying customer. Recurring platform revenue exists independent of game sales. Tier 2 fine-tuning viability assessed against accumulated telemetry data.

**H2 milestones:** Monster RPG shipped. Enterprise team-building product shipped. Third game selected against remaining capability gaps. Platform API stable for external developers. Open source self-hosting break-even calculated against actual H1 session volume and token consumption data. First co-founder or senior hire in place.

**H1 gate:** Two of three product proof signals met and two of three business proof signals met, per the framework in 01-Strategy-Document Section 7.

**Pre-build validation phase (required before significant production code is written):** Before engineering work begins on the production Nightcap system, a manual validation phase must produce qualifying results. This phase uses human-operated orchestration and off-the-shelf tools only; no production code. Minimum 10 facilitated sessions run with a human operator making pacing decisions, selecting clue targeting, and adjusting character behavior in real time. Sessions must include groups not involved in building the product. Minimum 2 sessions must produce the personalization perception signal: at least one player references something specifically calibrated to their group without being prompted. At least one session should use a format other than murder mystery to test whether the personalization effect is thesis-level or genre-level. The gate for entering production engineering is: qualifying sessions complete and personalization perception signal confirmed in at least 2 sessions. Sessions that complete successfully but produce no personalization perception signal require diagnosis before production engineering begins.

**Scope debt:** Dashboard at MVP is developer-facing only and is the first component to cut if time or scope pressure emerges; arc schema documentation and internal API architecture documentation serve the same near-term function at a fraction of the build cost; do not build dashboard polish before the consumer experience is validated. Knowledge graph schema at MVP is simple; extensibility path must be designed now. External developer documentation at MVP is not sufficient for a public API launch. Telemetry schema beyond MVP minimum must be designed in Chat 6. The pre-build Wizard-of-Oz validation phase is not optional scope that can be skipped to save time; skipping it means entering production engineering before knowing whether the personalization layer produces felt results in a real room.

## Visual Storyworld Roadmap

Visual creator tooling, internally referred to as Visual Storyworld, is staged across five phases. Phase 1 ships with platform MVP in H1 and includes inspection-only surfaces: live knowledge graph visualization, read-only arc structure view, live event stream, and character state inspection. Phase 2 ships during H2 prep and adds structured editing of existing primitives, including arc composition, character authoring, and content event templates. Phase 3 ships with H2 launch and adds live preview and runtime debugging. Phase 4 ships H2 mature to H3 early and adds a full visual flow editor. Phase 5 ships H3 plus and may add asset libraries, marketplace if pull exists, and AI-assisted authoring. Positioning is narrative middleware with extraordinary creator tooling, not a no-code platform.

## Business Model Architecture

Arcwright uses four pricing tiers: Free Developer, Indie, Studio, and Enterprise. Free Developer includes limited credits, full API access, rate limits, and prototyping use. Indie is designed for solo and small-team indie studios, with substantial included credits and community support. Studio is designed for studios of 5 to 50 people, with generous credits, priority support, and basic SLA. Enterprise uses custom annual contracts, SLA, eventually SOC 2 when achievable, and white-label or co-brand options. Action credits are abstracted as narrative actions, such as character dialogue generation, beat selection or transition, fate event evaluation, knowledge state query, or arc state update. Numeric prices remain deferred until Nightcap pricing is set.
