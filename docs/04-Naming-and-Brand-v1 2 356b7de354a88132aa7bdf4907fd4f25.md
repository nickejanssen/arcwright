## 2. Naming Reasoning

### Platform: Arcwright

Arcwright is a compound coinword: arc (narrative arc, structural span, precision) plus wright (craftsperson, as in playwright, wheelwright). Distinctive and ownable, carries craft and making imagery, earns its meaning over time rather than explaining itself upfront, and scales across all verticals and horizons without implying a single category. Passed all eight due-diligence checks with no blocking conflicts. The pretentiousness flag (the wright suffix is not universally known) was raised and consciously accepted.

### Brand Architecture: Soft Bifurcation

Arcwright is the platform-facing identity. Arcwright Studios is the consumer-facing publishing label for games. This absorbs the B2B trust concern around the Studios suffix without forcing premature legal or operational complexity. The company should not sell developer API infrastructure or enterprise products primarily under a Studios-only identity once those conversations begin. Legal entity name is deferred to counsel; operationally, the brand system treats Arcwright as platform and Arcwright Studios as studio label.

**Trademark strategy:** two filings under one entity: platform-facing brand under Class 9 or 42 (computer software), studio brand under Class 41 (entertainment).

### Game: Nightcap (pending clearance)

Retained as working title pending formal trademark counsel review. Two material conflicts identified: Studio Nightcap (active indie game studio in Pittsburgh making a murder mystery game) and NBA Nightcap (Amazon Prime Video, Class 41 entertainment). Counsel must clear or reject before any public launch materials use the name. Fallback committed: Revel.

### Game fallback: Revel

Selected for warmth, social energy, and clean conflict landscape. Revel is the party where the murder happens. Single word, adult, group-play energy. Still requires counsel review before launch, but no blocking conflicts found in initial research.

**Descriptor note:** Revel communicates party energy clearly but does not communicate mystery or whodunit format on its own. If Revel is activated as the primary name, consumer-facing marketing should pair it with a consistent subtitle or descriptor that communicates genre and format at first contact. Working examples: "Revel: The Murder Mystery Party" or "Revel: A Murder Mystery Game." The descriptor should be locked before any public launch materials are produced under the Revel name.

| Visual Storyworld | 9 or 42 if external-facing | Low | Not filed | Internal tooling name only for now; consider filing only if external-facing |

**Timing recommendation:** Engage trademark counsel before MVP launch. Clearance opinion costs $500 to $2,000. Filing costs $250 to $350 per class per mark via USPTO TEAS Plus. Do not wait until launch.

**Recommended approach:** Engage a trademark attorney directly for Nightcap clearance given the known conflicts. For Arcwright, a service like Markify or Trademarkia can run an initial search; attorney review before filing.

## 8. Brand Voice Notes

**What Arcwright Studios sounds like:** Confident without arrogance. Specific without jargon. Imaginative but grounded. The voice of someone who has built something they believe in and can tell you exactly why it works. Not breathless, not hyperbolic, not corporate.

**What Arcwright Studios does not sound like:** Gaming marketing copy. Enterprise SaaS. Startup press release voice. Overly warm and friendly. Ironic or self-deprecating.

**Nightcap voice (pending clearance):** Warm, knowing, adult. Like the host of a dinner party who has done this before and is genuinely delighted you showed up. Not spooky, not tryhard noir.
