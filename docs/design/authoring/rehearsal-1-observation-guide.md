## Rehearsal 1 Is The Whole-Session Test (D-092)

Rehearsal 1's purpose is to validate the **integrated night** — all six
beats, all minigame touchpoints, unified scoring, and the rhythm — not
any single mechanic. A rehearsal that only proved interrogation works
would be a *false pass*. The design authority for the whole night is
`couch-race-session-experience.md`; this guide is how you watch it run.
The observations below therefore cover voice, mechanics, product, AND
the whole-session integration (rhythm, minigames-as-evidence, one race).

## The Principle

You cannot debug what you did not watch for. Rehearsal 1 will be
overwhelming — real people, real couch, live engine — and every
observation you fail to plan will be replaced in memory by an
anecdote. This guide converts the design decisions into **planned
observations**, each with a trigger to stage, a
signal to watch, and a note format fast enough to keep up with a
live room.

## Ground Rules For The Session

- **You are not the host; Vesper is.** Every time you explain,
  clarify, or rescue, you contaminate the sample. Sit on your hands;
  write down what *made* you want to intervene — that list is the
  product backlog, verbatim.
- **Two cases minimum.** The bible's replay promise ("one more case"
  as the natural end state) is itself under test. The gap *between*
  cases — what the room does, unprompted, when case one ends — is the
  single most valuable minute of the night.
- **Capture format:** timestamp + beat + one line. No prose during
  play. Fill narrative after each case, not after each moment.

## The Twelve Planned Observations

### Voice and content (tonight's decisions under live fire)

**O1 — The first laugh.** Which Vesper line gets it, and which beat.
If it's not in the first two minutes, the cold-open jubilant set is
underperforming its job.

**O2 — The drop.** The room's face when the death lands. The shift is
the product's signature: you're watching for the *gap* — laughter to
silence — and whether anyone says a variant of "oh." Silence that
lasts into Beat 2 = working. Laughter that never breaks = the drop
line failed; note which co-d fired.

**O3 — The roast (D-081's live verdict).** Stage it: the rehearsal
script should guarantee at least two wrong accusations (brief players
beforehand that bold guessing is welcome — never that they should be
wrong on purpose). Watch the *accused player's* face, not the room's.
Laughing with = D-081 lands. A flinch the room laughs *over* =
recalibrate registers. This is Risk 3 from the master plan resolved
by evidence.

**O4 — Quoting.** Does anyone repeat a Vesper line back during play
or between cases? Quoted lines are the brand working. Note them
verbatim — they're also the marketing copy, free.

**O5 — The wink.** Does the room notice being noticed? Any reaction —
a glance exchanged, a laugh, a "did she just—" — validates the
once-per-session budget. No reaction = wrong beat placement, not
wrong idea; note which beat it fired in.

**O6 — The ordinary last line.** Watch for the photograph. If no
phone comes out at the held final frame across two cases, the
screenshot thesis needs work — either the line or the hold duration.

### Mechanics (the loop under real hands)

**O7 — First contradiction catch.** Time-to-first-flag, and the
catcher's *body* (the lean, the grab for the phone, the "wait wait
wait"). This is the headline mechanic's first live audience. If no
catch happens by mid-Grill unprompted, note whether the pacing engine
nudged and whether the nudge landed.

**O8 — Private information behavior.** The bible promises asymmetry
creates social play. Watch: do phone-holders *tell* the room their
private evidence, hoard it, or trade it? All three are fine; which
one dominates shapes AW-280's direction lock (testimony-style clues
reward telling; ledger-style rewards hoarding).

**O9 — The twist re-audit.** At Beat 4, watch for visible re-ranking:
players verbally demoting their suspect ("okay so if the alibi's
gone—"). The twist's authored function is *reordering suspicion*; if
the room shrugs and keeps its theories, the family that fired
(record which) under-punished confidence.

**O10 — The race feel.** Last Call: does the countdown change
posture? Leaning, interrupting, faster asks = the race is alive.
Politeness continuing = the scarcity design (question budgets, timer
visibility) needs teeth. Note the exact countdown mark where behavior
changed, if it did.

### Product (the night as a whole)

**O11 — The gap between cases.** The unprompted verdict. "Again" with
no prompt = the core loop is proven. Phone-checking, bathroom drift,
"what time is it" = session length or Beat 6 pacing is wrong. Time
the gap from reveal to first "again" (or to first exit behavior).

**O12 — The retell test (next day).** Ask one attendee, tomorrow,
unprompted-style: "what happened last night?" What survives 24 hours
is the product. If they retell a Vesper line, a catch, or the twist —
those are the load-bearing features. If they retell setup friction,
that's the backlog's top item.

### Whole-session integration (the thread we almost lost)

**O13 — The rhythm.** Track energy across all six beats. Does it
alternate (cinematic Pour → fast Scene → focused Grill → jolt Twist →
loud Last Call → cathartic Truth), or does it flatline or lull? Note
the exact beat where energy dropped, if it did. A night that sags in
the middle is a pacing/rhythm defect, not a content one.

**O14 — Minigame: evidence or detour?** When a minigame fires (Scene:
Crime Scene Smash; Twist: Evidence Locker), watch whether players treat
it as *earning their evidence* or as an interruption from the mystery.
Do they carry the minigame result into the investigation ("I won so I
got the good clue"), or forget it the moment it ends? Detour-feel = the
evidence-economy rule (session-experience doc) isn't landing. This is
the direct test of the founder's match-3 worry.

**O15 — One race or three scoreboards?** At the scoreboard (Truth), ask
players *why* they think they won or lost. If they can name it
("I caught the most lies and accused right") the unified scoring reads.
If they're confused about how minigame points, catches, and accusation
combined, scoring is fragmented — the integration failed even if each
part worked.

**O16 — Does the mystery drive the night?** The meta-observation. Strip
away the narration and minigames: did solving the case feel like the
point, or did it feel like activities loosely wrapped in a story? If a
player says "I didn't really care who did it, but the games were fun,"
that is a *fail* for a mystery product, however fun the night was —
and the most important thing to catch.

## What NOT To Fix During Rehearsal Week

The AW-267 lesson, encoded: observations feed the *debrief*, and the
debrief feeds prioritized remediation through the normal gates. No
same-night line edits, no mid-week register changes, no "quick fix"
to a library between case one and case two — every observation above
is only valid against stable content. One rehearsal, one content
version, one debrief.

## Output Contract

The rehearsal produces exactly three artifacts: (1) the timestamped
observation log (this guide's twelve, plus surprises), (2) the
intervention-urge list, (3) the 24-hour retell notes. AW-286's
debrief gate consumes all three; remediation gets planned there, not
on the couch.
