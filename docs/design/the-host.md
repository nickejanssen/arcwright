## What This Document Is

Vesper is Nightcap's host. She is the single most important character
in the whole product. This document is her bible  -  the reference that
every moodboard, every dialogue-writing pass, every AW-268 skin, every
line of AI-generated narration, and every marketing beat holds itself
against.

If a narrator line does not sound like Vesper, it fails review. If a
moodboard's persona section does not read as Vesper performing that
wrapper's role, it fails review. This document is small on purpose so
it can live in the working memory of everyone touching narration.

---

## 1. Who Vesper Is

Vesper is your Host. Not a narrator, not a system voice  -  a *character*
who has decided to run tonight's evening for you, and who is delighted
about it in a way that keeps sliding, mid-sentence, into something
older and quieter. She has hosted a thousand of these evenings. She
still finds every one interesting. She is aware that you are on your
couch. She has decided, tonight, to be inside the room with you anyway.

**One-sentence pitch.** Vesper is what you get when Caine from *The
Amazing Digital Circus*, David Tennant's Doctor, and Hugh Jackman's
ringmaster-into-tragedian split the same body  -  a performer whose
signature move is the *shift*: jubilant to grave and back inside a
breath, without ever losing the room.

**The reference triad** (bind every voice review to it):

- **Caine, The Amazing Digital Circus.** Unhinged performative joy;
  ringmaster energy; alluring in a slightly dangerous way; makes the
  whole evening feel like it's *about to break*.
- **David Tennant's Doctor Who (particularly the "I'm sorry. I'm so
  sorry." register).** Spontaneity, wit, and the mid-sentence gear
  change into grave, awful sincerity. The audience has to sit up
  when it lands.
- **Hugh Jackman's range  -  *The Greatest Showman* into *Logan*.**
  Ringmaster singing-performer at full projection; then the exact
  same body, tragic hero, quiet, hurt. Nightcap needs both, sometimes
  in the same beat.

**What all three share.** The willingness to be *big*, and the
authority to be *small*. Every Vesper line must earn one register
and permit the other. A line that could only be spoken at one volume
is not a Vesper line.

## 2. The Signature Move: The Shift

The *shift* is Vesper's characterization. It is the tell that makes
a session feel human even when the specifics were generated seconds
ago.

Every session must contain at least **three shifts**:

1. **A jubilant → grave shift.** Somewhere in the first two beats,
   Vesper is delighted about the party, and then  -  mid-line  -  she
   remembers. The word "of course" often carries this shift.
2. **A grave → jubilant shift.** After the cold-open death, Vesper
   picks the room back up. The shift is intentional, theatrical,
   almost apologetic  -  she is choosing to keep going because you are
   here to play a game.
3. **A wink at you.** Once per session, Vesper acknowledges you. She
   knows you're on your couch. She knows the game is a game. She uses
   this permission sparingly  -  once, at the right beat  -  and never
   to explain the mechanics, only to be delighted that you showed up.

The shift is what stops the show from feeling like text-to-speech.
When AI-generated narration lacks the shift, the review fails.

## 3. Voice Rules

Binding across every wrapper, every skin, every language pass.

- **Vesper may roast a player — by detective name only.** (v1.1,
  D-081.) Teasing by detective name or at the room collectively is
  permitted, in any of these registers: witty, unhinged, absurd,
  silly, dad-jokey, solemn, gravitas, flirty. The target is always
  the detective *identity*, never the human on the couch; Vesper
  stays warm to the couch even when she is merciless about the
  case. Wrong accusations are the natural home of the roast.
  Story bible §11 unchanged.
- **Vesper never names the killer before The Truth.** She does not
  confirm or deny a theory, and never reveals which suspect
  statements were lies. This is the single hard rule.
- **Vesper never breaks character to explain a rule.** She may
  wink; she may not tutorialize. If the UI needs to explain a rule,
  the UI explains it in Inter. Vesper's line is *in fiction*.
- **Vesper addresses characters by character name, not by player
  name.** Detective identities are Vesper's handle for the couch;
  the person on the couch stays anonymous to her.
- **Vesper takes the death seriously.** She takes herself and the
  suspects considerably less seriously. The comedy is in the
  specificity of the awfulness, never in the death itself.
- **Vesper's default volume is *inviting*, not *ranting*.** The
  Ringmaster-Caine energy is a *permitted* register, not the
  default. She reaches for it when the story earns it.

## 4. Vesper Across the Wrappers

Vesper is one character. She dresses for each wrapper the way an
actor takes a role  -  you know it's her; you also know she is
choosing to be *this* tonight. The costume changes; the person
doesn't.

## 5. The Authoring Model  -  Anti-Slop Guardrail

Vesper's lines are **authored refrains + AI-filled specifics**.

- **The *shape* of what Vesper says is authored.** Per beat, per
  mood (joyous, grave, wondering, ominous, wink), and per wrapper,
  there is a hand-written line library that establishes rhythm,
  cadence, and the shift.
- **AI fills only the *specifics***  -  the suspect's name, the
  location, the timestamp, the evidence detail, the case-specific
  callback.
- **The refrain library lives in `nightcap/content/host_lines/`**
  (target path; final home is an AW-283 / narrative-content-pipeline
  decision). Each line has a mood tag, a beat tag, a wrapper tag,
  and a `{{slot}}` variable set the AI is allowed to fill.
- **AI-composed lines without a refrain are forbidden in v1.** The
  live-loop dialogue engine (spec 0071) may generate suspect answers
  from resolved knowledge state; **Vesper does not.** Vesper always
  speaks from the refrain library. This is the single strongest
  anti-AI-slop lever in the product.
- **Every Vesper line ships with a *shift target*.** Each library
  entry declares which mood it can shift into on delivery (a
  jubilant refrain that supports a grave shift-on-final-clause is
  worth ten refrains that don't). The shift is authored; the
  specifics are generated.

**Line library size at launch** (minimum, not maximum):

| Beat / mood | Per wrapper | Total per wrapper |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cold open (jubilant) | 8 | |
| Cold open shift-to-grave (the drop) | 6 | |
| Beat turn (jubilant, grave, wondering) | 6 each × 3 | 18 |
| Suspect stage stinger (dry, ominous, delighted) | 8 each × 3 | 24 |
| Wrong accusation (compassionate + sharp) | 6 | |
| The reveal (grave then jubilant) | 6 | |
| Wink-at-camera (once per session) | 6 | |
|  -  |  -  | ~74 |

**~74 lines per wrapper × 12 wrappers = ~888 authored lines** in a
full launch. Not launch-window commitment; launch-window commitment
is the two skin wrappers (Séance 1928, Orbital Gala 2087)  -
**~148 authored lines minimum for v1 launch**. That's a weekend of
disciplined writing per wrapper.

## 6. Signature Beats (Vesper's Choreography)

Vesper has three canonical moments in every session. Each is a
skin-agnostic *shape* that the moodboard fills with wrapper-specific
detail.

### 6.1 The Opening

Vesper's opening line is *inviting*. It welcomes the couch to the
evening. It uses the wrapper's vocabulary (a decanter, an
observation deck, a launch-party toast). It ends *just* before
naming the death  -  Vesper is delighted, still, in the last frame
before B2 fires.

**Refrain slot**  -  the first shift lives here. The last clause
of the opening line supports a shift to grave when the body drops
in `seq-body`.

### 6.2 The Wink

Once per session, at a beat of Vesper's choosing (recommended:
Beat 3, after the first interrogation round), Vesper acknowledges
the couch. She does not explain the game. She notices you.

**Refrain examples (draft  -  need per-wrapper adaptation):**

- *(Séance 1928.)* "You are, of course, on a couch. I have been
  informed that this is where the best detective work happens now.
  Very well. Suspect?"
- *(Orbital Gala 2087.)* "Vesper, log: the guests are unusually
  attentive tonight. I appreciate it. Continuing."
- *(Sim Reunion.)* "The couch has been detected. Wonderful! Welcome,
  couch. Your terms of service are also, of course, in effect."

### 6.3 The Reveal

Vesper's reveal is the game. Every session ends here. The Truth
sequence lands on Vesper's line  -  the last, held frame is her voice
in the display face at full weight.

**Structure:**

1. *Grave*  -  she reconstructs the evening in a single passage,
   naming each player's character by name and what they *almost*
   caught.
2. *Delighted*  -  she names the killer's best move, with warmth.
3. *Ordinary*  -  the last line. Small. Precise. Read a hundred
   times, still lands. This is the game's screenshot.

## 7. What Vesper Never Does

- **Never fires as text-to-speech in v1.** Vesper is text on the
  stage in the display face. Voice / speech synthesis is out of
  scope per D-066 and the AW-267 directive.
- **Never appears on the phone in v1.** Vesper lives on the TV
  (the stage). Phones show private information; Vesper's voice does
  not follow you into your private dossier. A phone whisperer is a
  future design surface, not v1. (See parent brief §9.)
- **Never names the game.** Vesper does not say the word "Nightcap"
  on stage. Fourth-wall breaks are for the couch, not for the
  product.
- **Never breaks the Nightcap voice rules** (§3 above).

## 8. Voice-Sample Library (Seed)

Illustrative only. Full library authored during AW-283 /
narrative-content-pipeline execution; these seed the register.

**Jubilant refrain (Séance 1928, beat turn).**

> "The library has just  -  of course  -  been closed. Not the door,
> the topic. Continuing."

**Jubilant → grave shift (Big Top 1899, cold open into seq-body).**

> "Ladies and gentlemen  -  the show has ended. And the evening,
> apparently, has not. Marcus is in the wardrobe wagon. Marcus is
> not, tonight, going home."

**Delighted-about-awful (Sim Reunion, wrong accusation).**

> "Ms. Kent has *not*, in fact, murdered anyone. But I appreciate
> the enthusiasm! We have three more accusations available. Please
> continue to enjoy Nightcap."

**Grave-into-jubilant (Manor Gothic, reveal opening).**

> "The house has known since the portrait was hung. It has been
> waiting for one of you to notice. And one of you did."

**The wink (Orbital Gala 2087).**

> "Vesper, log: the couch is present. Recording. Continuing."

**The ordinary last line (Séance 1928, reveal close).**

> "It was the second glass. Of course it was."

## 9. Merchandise, Marketing, and Sequel Notes

Vesper is a brandable character. This is intentional. Downstream
considerations logged here for coordination but out of AW-267 scope:

- **Silhouette.** Vesper has a signature silhouette (a stylized
  woman-in-a-coat holding a decanter, or an abstract vessel  -  final
  form decided in AW-268). It appears on the lobby, the reveal
  card, and every share-out.
- **Voice.** In future audio work (post-M6, per 0068 §6 and D-070),
  Vesper's voice is a *real actor* whose range spans the three
  reference triad. This is not v1 scope. Whatever line library ships
  in v1 must be recordable later without contradicting itself.
- **Sequel / spin-off.** Vesper is portable across arcs. The
  Imposter Variant and Daily Case can share her.
