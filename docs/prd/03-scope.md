# Scope

## MVP Definition and Milestone Map

**Scope clarity:** "Platform MVP" at this stage means the internal engine that runs Nightcap, with architecture clean and documented enough that a technical co-founder joining post-MVP can understand every decision without a guided tour. It is not an externally packaged API product, not a polished developer-facing service, and not a platform open to third-party developers. The done-criterion "documented well enough for a technical co-founder" means internal architecture documentation, not a public developer experience. This distinction determines where build time goes during H1.

**MVP is done when all of the following are true:** Nightcap is playable end-to-end by a group of real human players not involved in building it, with a v1 floor of four human players and scaling up with additional human players. Two- and three-player sessions require interrogatable AI participants and are deferred to v1.1. The session completes through the canonical eight Nightcap Story Circle beats, as defined in `docs/story-bibles/nightcap-murder-mystery.md` Section 4, including the reveal. Knowledge state enforcement works. Session persistence works. The provider-agnostic model routing abstraction layer is in place. Structured session telemetry is live logging minimum required signals. The platform API is documented well enough for a technical co-founder to read the architecture without explanation. AI credit tracking is live and the founder knows per-session gross margin at each supported human player count. Content safety rails are active. A second arc schema has been designed, and after M6 proof it is built as a minimal executable product to validate platform reuse by execution. At least one adversarial playtest round has been completed, specifically designed to probe content safety rails, dark-content edge cases, and player attempts to break tone, embarrass other players, or inject prohibited content; results reviewed and blocking issues resolved before first real-user sessions begin. The solo-founder critical path has been honored: the developer-facing dashboard and arc authoring interface are the lowest-priority MVP components and are deferred until the consumer experience is validated; the critical path for solo delivery is arc execution engine, session management, knowledge graph, Nightcap game integration, safety rails, basic cost tracking, and structured telemetry.

**H3+ North Star:** Arcwright is platform-first. External developers ship experiences on the infrastructure. Tier 2 proprietary infrastructure is active for at least some model call categories.

**H2 gate:** External developer closed beta opens only after two gate conditions are met: at least five external developers are building non-Nightcap experiences and Nightcap has produced a profitability signal. The specific profitability threshold (revenue level, unit economics, recurring revenue, or combined signal) is deferred until Nightcap pricing is set. Monster RPG shipped or in final development. Enterprise team-building product has at least one paying customer. Recurring platform revenue exists independent of game sales. Tier 2 fine-tuning viability assessed against accumulated telemetry data.

**H2 milestones:** Monster RPG shipped. Enterprise team-building product shipped. Third game selected against remaining capability gaps. Platform API stable for external developers. Open source self-hosting break-even calculated against actual H1 session volume and token consumption data. First co-founder or senior hire in place.

**H1 gate:** Two of three product proof signals met and two of three business proof signals met, per the framework in 01-Strategy-Document Section 7.

**Pre-build validation phase (required before significant production code is written):** Before engineering work begins on the production Nightcap system, a manual validation phase must produce qualifying results. This phase uses human-operated orchestration and off-the-shelf tools only; no production code. Minimum 10 facilitated sessions run with a human operator making pacing decisions, selecting clue targeting, and adjusting character behavior in real time. Sessions must include groups not involved in building the product. Minimum 2 sessions must produce the personalization perception signal: at least one player references something specifically calibrated to their group without being prompted. At least one session should use a format other than murder mystery to test whether the personalization effect is thesis-level or genre-level. The gate for entering production engineering is: qualifying sessions complete and personalization perception signal confirmed in at least 2 sessions. Sessions that complete successfully but produce no personalization perception signal require diagnosis before production engineering begins.

**Scope debt:** Dashboard at MVP is developer-facing only and is the first component to cut if time or scope pressure emerges; arc schema documentation and internal API architecture documentation serve the same near-term function at a fraction of the build cost; do not build dashboard polish before the consumer experience is validated. Knowledge graph schema at MVP is simple; extensibility path must be designed now. External developer documentation at MVP is not sufficient for a public API launch. Telemetry schema beyond MVP minimum must be designed in Chat 6. The pre-build Wizard-of-Oz validation phase is not optional scope that can be skipped to save time; skipping it means entering production engineering before knowing whether the personalization layer produces felt results in a real room.

## Visual Storyworld Roadmap

Visual creator tooling, internally referred to as Visual Storyworld, is staged across five phases. The H1 proof-critical Phase 1 requirement is the live knowledge graph inspection surface. Read-only arc structure view, live event stream, and character state inspection may ship as logs or defer until after proof if needed to protect the solo-founder critical path. Phase 2 ships during H2 prep and adds structured editing of existing primitives, including arc composition, character authoring, and content event templates. Phase 3 ships with H2 launch and adds live preview and runtime debugging. Phase 4 ships H2 mature to H3 early and adds a full visual flow editor. Phase 5 ships H3 plus and may add asset libraries, marketplace if pull exists, and AI-assisted authoring. Positioning is narrative middleware with extraordinary creator tooling, not a no-code platform.

## Business Model Architecture

Arcwright uses four pricing tiers for the future developer platform: Free Developer, Indie, Studio, and Enterprise. Free Developer includes limited credits, full API access, rate limits, and prototyping use. Indie is designed for solo and small-team indie studios, with substantial included credits and community support. Studio is designed for studios of 5 to 50 people, with generous credits, priority support, and basic SLA. Enterprise uses custom annual contracts, SLA, eventually SOC 2 when achievable, and white-label or co-brand options. Action credits are abstracted as narrative actions, such as character dialogue generation, beat selection or transition, fate event evaluation, knowledge state query, or arc state update. Nightcap's temporary pricing north star is host-pays-per-session, anchored above the roughly $30 static murder-mystery-kit price point. Credit packs are a later retention or average-order optimization only if repeat purchase appears. The engine must support per-session charging and per-session cost tracking. Numeric launch price remains deferred until real per-session AI cost and early willingness-to-pay signal are available.
